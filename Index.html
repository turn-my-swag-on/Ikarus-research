The Ikarus Framework

How we separate moat durability from current financial performance — and why that distinction determines long-duration returns.

The Core Premise

Financial markets are effective at pricing current execution — revenue growth, margin expansion, earnings beats. They are systematically slow at pricing structural permanence — the mechanism that will still generate cash flows 5, 10, and 15 years from now. The Ikarus framework scores permanence explicitly, then compares it to today's price. When a company with durable structural advantages trades at a fraction of its structural peers, that gap is the opportunity.

What a "Structural Moat" Means

A structural moat exists when a competitor's rational best response to displacing you is to decide it isn't worth trying. This happens via three mechanisms: (1) Royalty compounding — your IP earns silently on every device shipped by your customer's customers, with no incremental cost; (2) Workflow lock-in — your technology is embedded in a customer's design process such that switching costs 12–18 months and schedule risk; (3) Process exclusivity — you own a physical manufacturing process that takes competitors years and billions to replicate.

What It Is Not

Structural moats are distinct from execution advantages — being the fastest-growing, most profitable, or best-run company in a category. Execution is real and valuable, but it is priced. Structural permanence means the moat compounds automatically — more devices shipped, more customers adopting an ecosystem, more generations of a standard — without the company needing to win the next product cycle competition from scratch.

Pattern 1 · Royalty Engine

When a company captures recurring, per-unit payments from devices it did not manufacture — and customers cannot ship without its IP — revenue compounds as a direct function of global industry volume. The more devices shipped worldwide, the more is earned without incremental cost or competitive re-qualification. Active in: CEVA & AIP.

Pattern 2 · Workflow Lock-in

When a company's technology is embedded in a customer's chip design or engineering workflow, switching carries 12–18 months of re-qualification cost and schedule risk. Once it becomes the internal standard, displacement is economically irrational even when alternatives exist. Active in: AIP (full); ALAB (partial).

Pattern 3 · Process Fortress

When a company owns a hard-to-replicate physical manufacturing process combined with low supply chain fragility, competitors face a multi-year, multi-billion-dollar barrier to entry. This pattern requires both process exclusivity AND low financial fragility to fully activate. Approaching in: LITE (fragility prevents full activation).

Warren Buffett Lens

Applying Buffett's core investment principles — pricing power, economic moats, owner earnings, and margin of safety — to the five companies.

"It's far better to buy a wonderful company at a fair price than a fair company at a wonderful price." — 1989

CEVA and AIP are the "wonderful companies" — 88–92% gross margins, royalty-based compounding, zero supply chain risk. They trade at fair-to-cheap prices (3.3× and 11.5× EV/Sales). CRDO and LITE are strong businesses priced at "wonderful prices" that demand flawless execution. Buffett's preference in this cohort would be unambiguous.

"The single most important decision in evaluating a business is pricing power." — 2011

Pure IP licensors have the ultimate pricing power — customers cannot ship devices without licensing their IP. CEVA commands 68% global wireless IP market share. Once Arteris interconnect IP is embedded in an SoC design flow, re-qualification costs 12–18 months of engineering effort. This is the "toll bridge" Buffett describes — unavoidable, recurring, and expanding with industry volume.

"In business, I look for economic castles protected by unbreachable moats." — 1993

The Royalty Engine and Workflow Lock-in patterns are the formal systematization of Buffett's "unbreachable moat" concept. AIP and CEVA pass this test through mechanisms that compound silently — every device shipped globally that uses their IP generates recurring revenue without incremental cost or competitive re-qualification. Time is the investor's ally, not adversary.
